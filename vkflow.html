Payment Architecture FAQ

Why use a dedicated Merchant Account over Stripe or Square?
Aggregators like Stripe and Square are excellent for early-stage startups, but as your transaction volume scales, they become a liability. Aggregators group your funds with millions of others, leading to aggressive, algorithmic account freezes with no warning. A dedicated Merchant Account, engineered by V&K Partners, provides you with your own unique Merchant Identification Number (MID). This grants you absolute control over your funds, bespoke interchange pricing, and direct access to banking underwriters—eliminating the risk of sudden capital locks.
What is ACH Processing and why is it vital for B2B?
Automated Clearing House (ACH) processing allows you to pull funds directly from a client's bank account. While credit cards charge an average of 2.9% + $0.30 per transaction (costing you $2,900 on a $100,000 invoice), ACH transactions typically cost a flat fee ranging from $0.50 to a few dollars, regardless of the transaction size. For high-ticket B2B operations, embedding ACH rails is the single fastest way to immediately increase your profit margin.
How does V&K handle PCI Compliance?
We utilize advanced cloud tokenization and hosted payment fields. When your client enters their credit card data, it bypasses your servers entirely and goes directly to our Level 1 PCI-DSS compliant vault. Your system receives a secure "token" to process future transactions. Because your infrastructure never touches raw cardholder data, your PCI compliance scope is drastically reduced, saving you thousands in annual audit fees.
Can VK Flow integrate with our existing CRM and accounting software?
Absolutely. The VK Flow architecture is built on robust, RESTful APIs designed for seamless integration. We connect directly into major enterprise resource planning (ERP) systems, CRMs (Salesforce, HubSpot), and accounting ledgers (NetSuite, QuickBooks) to ensure real-time reconciliation and automated ledger updates without manual data entry.
