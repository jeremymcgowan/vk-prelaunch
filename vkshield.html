Managed IT Architecture FAQ

What is the difference between Break-Fix and Managed IT?
Break-fix IT means you only call a technician when something is already broken—resulting in lost revenue, idle employees, and unpredictable hourly bills. Managed IT (The VK Shield approach) operates on a proactive model. We deploy monitoring agents across your entire network to identify failing hard drives, patch security vulnerabilities, and resolve issues before they ever impact your operations.
How does Zero-Touch Device Provisioning work?
When you hire a new employee, we bypass your local office entirely. V&K orders the hardware directly from the manufacturer (e.g., Apple). Using corporate-level deployment programs (like Apple Business Manager), we link the device serial number to your cloud console. The laptop is shipped directly to the employee’s home in a sealed box. The moment they connect to Wi-Fi, the device automatically downloads your security policies, configures their email, and installs required software without IT ever touching the physical machine.
Can you ensure our network is FINRA or HIPAA compliant?
Yes. Regulatory compliance is not just about software; it requires rigid architectural protocols. We configure your cloud environments, email servers, and physical devices to meet strict data retention rules, enforce encrypted transit, mandate complex access controls, and generate the necessary audit logs required to pass regulatory scrutiny.
What happens if our primary servers are hit with Ransomware?
If a threat penetrates the primary network, our immutable backup protocols activate. "Immutable" means the backup data cannot be altered or deleted, even by an administrator or malicious software. We simply sever the infected environment, wipe it, and restore your entire operational data from the secure, redundant cloud vault—typically resulting in near-zero data loss.
