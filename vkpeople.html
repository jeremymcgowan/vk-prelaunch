Workforce Architecture FAQ

What is an Employer of Record (EOR)?
An Employer of Record (EOR) is a third-party organization that becomes the legal employer of your international workforce. V&K Partners handles all payroll, taxes, visa sponsorships, and benefits administration, while you maintain complete control over the employee’s daily tasks, performance reviews, and operational duties. It is the fastest way to expand globally without the massive cost of setting up a foreign corporate entity.
Why shouldn't we just hire everyone as an Independent Contractor?
Many startups attempt this to save money, but it is highly illegal if the worker is treated like an employee. If a contractor works set hours, uses company-provided equipment, or is integral to your core business, labor boards view them as full-time employees. If audited, your enterprise will be forced to pay years of back-taxes, uncollected benefits, and severe penalties. Utilizing our EOR infrastructure eliminates this risk entirely.
How does multi-currency payroll funding actually work?
Instead of your finance team executing 40 different wire transfers in 12 different currencies, V&K aggregates your total payroll cost. You receive one single invoice in US Dollars (USD). Once you fund that invoice via ACH or wire, our treasury engine automatically executes the foreign exchange (FX) conversions and distributes the exact correct fiat amount into each employee's local bank account on payday.
Who owns the Intellectual Property created by an EOR employee?
Your enterprise does. A critical part of our localized employment contracts is an airtight, region-specific Intellectual Property Assignment clause. From the moment the employee signs the contract, all code, designs, and proprietary assets they create are instantly and legally transferred to your core holding company.
